## Ingredients

2 dressed lemon sole , 2 sardines, scaled and gutted , 1 tsp tamarind paste , 4 slices ginger , 1 tsp salt , 1 litre water , 10 dried chillies, soaked and snipped into 1 cm pieces , 2 fresh red chillies, sliced , 80 g shallots, sliced (or a 50/50 mix of red/brown onion) , 2 stalks lemongrass, sliced , 1 cm ginger, sliced , 1 cm turmeric, sliced (or 1 tsp ground turmeric) , 2 garlic cloves, sliced , 10 g shrimp paste (belacan) , 4 tbsp oil , 900 ml fish stock , 2 stalks daun kesum (laksa leaves/Vietnamese coriander) , 1 tbsp dried bunga kantan (torch ginger), optional , 2 large limau purut (Thai Makrut lime leaves) , 2 tbsp tamarind paste , 2 tsp salt , 1 tbsp sugar , 150 ml thick coconut milk , 400 g fresh laksa noodles (substitute udon if needed) , 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced , 2/3 cup pineapple, cut into strips , 2/3 cup mint leaves , 1 cucumber, shredded , 1 red chilli, finely sliced , 4 limes, halved and seeded , Hei ko (prawn paste), diluted with a bit of hot water, optional

*A fragrant and creamy Malaysian noodle soup featuring a rich fish and coconut gravy, aromatic spices, and fresh garnishes. Also known as Penang Laksa Lemak, this family recipe is less tangy than Assam Laksa and packed with vibrant Southeast Asian flavors.*
