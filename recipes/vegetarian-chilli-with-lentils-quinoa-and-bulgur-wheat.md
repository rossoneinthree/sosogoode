## Ingredients

### For the Chilli
- Olive or rapeseed oil
- 2 medium onions, peeled and finely chopped
- 8 cloves garlic, peeled and finely chopped
- Thumb-size piece fresh ginger, peeled and finely chopped
- 1 red or green chilli, finely chopped
- 1 tsp good chilli powder
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tbsp cumin seeds, bashed
- 1 heaped tbsp chipotle paste
- 3 Ã— 400g tins chopped tomatoes
- 2 tins cooked Puy lentils (about 500g), drained
- 100g bulgur wheat
- 100g quinoa (red or white)
- 1 Ã— 400g tin small beans (haricot, black or black-eyed)
- 1 heaped tbsp good cocoa powder
- 1-2 litres vegetable stock
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

### For the Thyme Oil
- 6 tbsp olive oil
- 2 green or red chillies, finely chopped
- Small bunch fresh thyme

### To Serve
- Salted natural yoghurt
- Warmed corn tortillas

## Method

### Cook the Onions and Garlic
1. Get out your biggest pot and place it on a medium heat. Add a splash of olive or rapeseed oil and cook the 2 chopped onions, 8 cloves chopped garlic, thumb of chopped ginger and finely chopped chilli for 10 minutes, until soft and sweet.

### Add the Spices
2. Now add the 1 teaspoon of chilli powder, 2 teaspoons of smoked paprika, 1 tablespoon of cumin seeds and stir around in the pan for a minute or two.

### Add Everything Else
3. Then add the tablespoon of chipotle paste, 3 tins of chopped tomatoes, 2 drained tins of Puy lentils, 100g of bulgur wheat, 100g of quinoa, 1 can of black beans with their liquid and a tablespoon of cocoa. Add 1 litre of the stock to start with, and keep the rest on hand to add as needed if the chilli starts to look a bit dry.

### Simmer
4. Bring to a gentle boil, then turn the heat down to low and leave to simmer and blip away for 30-35 minutes, until the lentils are cooked and the chilli is deep and flavoursome. Add the remaining litre of stock as needed.

### Make the Thyme Oil
5. Make a thyme oil by mixing the 6 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 finely chopped chillies and the leaves from a small bunch of thyme with a sprinkling of salt and pepper.

### Finish the Chilli
6. Taste your chilli and add a little more salt and pepper if you like. Serve in bowls, topped with a little yoghurt, a drizzle of thyme oil and some warmed corn tortillas for scooping.

## Info

- **Cook:** 60-70 mins
- **Total:** 60-70 mins
- **Serves:** 4

### Storage & Leftovers

- The cooked chilli can be kept covered in the fridge for up to 5 days.
- It can be kept in the freezer for up to 4 months.
- Reheat in a pan with a little extra water until piping hot.

*Hearty vegetarian chilli packed with lentils, quinoa, bulgur wheat and beans. Deep, smoky flavour from chipotle and cocoa. Perfect with yoghurt and warm tortillas.*
